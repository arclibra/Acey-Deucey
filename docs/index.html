Acey-deucey

also known as In-Between, Sheets or Maverick

A simple card game that involves betting between the cards.

Introduction

Rules:

Before the action, each player must add their ante into the pot. Two cards are then dealt face-up to one player.

That player then bets from nothing to the amount that is in the pot at the time whether or not the third card will numerically fall in between the first two.

If the third card falls in between the two other cards, the bettor takes the amount he bet out of the pot; if the third card falls outside of the two other cards, the bettor must add what he bet to the pot; and if the third card matches the numerical value of one of the other two cards, the bettor must add to the pot double what he bet. If two cards of the same value come up, e.g. 2,2 the bettor picks if the next card will be higher or lower and bets. If the next card is the same as the last two, i.e. a 2, the bettor must triple his/her bet.

Player get 8 hearts between King and Ace.
Player wins the bets.

Variations

A variation is to split the cards if two end cards are the same value. This requires the bettor to ante in for two hands and the dealer would draw one more card under each of the end cards. After this, the same rules apply.
Some new rules that increase payouts and betting:

  • Blind Pot

    Bet the pot before your 2 outside cards are placed. In the case of a win, you win the pot. If you are outside of your cards you lose half the pot. If you post you have to pay the full amount of the pot.

  • AutoPot

    Any A-2 played as your oustside cards automatically make the player bet the full amount of the pot.

  • Post Bet

    You can bet that your inside card will match one of your outside cards. In the case of this you win the full pot and in addition each player pays an equal percentage to match the pot. Therefore, the player that post would win 2x the pot. If the player bets the post and misses he owes half the amount of the pot to the pot.

  • Satan 6s

    Any player that gets 6-6-6 as their cards must pay 6x the amount of the pot to the pot.

General Strategy

Acey-duecey, though at its first sight looks straight forward, is actually not as intuitive as many other card game.

For example: You have been dealt with An Ace and an eight. There are 7 card out of 13 cards is fall in betweens these two number


Should you go for it?

And if so, how many bets should you make?

Answer:

No, you should not even bet anything for it. Here's why.

In order to know whether one's would came out winning or losing on any odds. One needs to understand concept of Expected value (EV).

Expected Value

By definition, Expected Value is closely defined as such

The amount a player can expect to win or lose if they were to place a bet on the same odds many times over, calculated through a simple equation multiplying your probability of winning with the amount you could win per bet, and subtracting the probability of losing multiplied by the amount lost per bet.

With that, we formulate a way to calculate one's win/lose by this formula.

EV = p(W) - p(L)

where
EV = Expected value,
p(W) = probability of Winning * payout
p(L) = probability of losing * lost bet.

Testing Scenario

Time to use this knowledge to evaluate each scenario. We will be using example previously given, (Low Ace and Eight).
and calculate the probability of winning and losing step by step.

Winning scenarios:

The expected value of Winning shall be calculate as such:

Chances of getting in-between Low Ace and Eight cards: 6 cards (two to seven) out of 13 cards (each suit has the same probability if we only consider the number)

Payout rate: 1

p(W) = 6/13 * 1

p(W) = 6/13

We get 6/13 as our positive Expected Value (EV) from our probability of winning.

Losing scenarios:

In losing scenario it shall be a little different.
Since we were expected to pay twice the bet amount when we have the same card as the previous two card display on table, and only the amount we bet when we fall out of the range.
We will need to calculate both scenario saperately.

First scenario, fall out of the range:

The expected value of Losing when fall out of range shall be calculate as such:

Chances of getting out of range Ace and Eight cards:
5 cards (nine to King cards) out of 13 cards (each suit has the same probability if we only consider the number)

Paying rate: 1

p(L) = 5/13 * 1

p(L) = 5/13

We get 5/13 as our negetive Expected Value (EV) from our probability of losing.

Second scenario, The expected losing value when player card land on either Ace or eight.

Chances of getting Ace or Eight cards:
2 cards (nine to King cards) out of 13 cards (each suit has the same probability if we only consider the number, to simplified things.)

Paying rate: 2

p(L) = 2/13 * 2

p(L) = 4/13

We get 4/13 as our negetive Expected Value (EV) from our probability of losing when player card land on either Ace or eight.

Conclusion

All the scenario have been considered, now we just add up all the positive and negetive scenario and we get:

Expected Value:

EV of Win: 6/13

EV of Lose: 5/13 + 4/13 = 9/13

Total EV = 6/13 - 9/13) = - 3/13

We actally get -3/13 EV when bet on scenario Low Ace to Eight. A bad move. A negetive EV means that you will lost your bet more in the long run rather than winning.
